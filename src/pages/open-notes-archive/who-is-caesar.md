First, let’s clear the air and describe who or what Caesar is not.

Caesar is not any government, or even a cohort of them. Caesar is not Big Brother. Caesar is not capitalism, socialism, or communism. Caesar is not a ruler, or bureaucracy, or elected officials. Caesar is not a CEO, or an oligarchy of the most valuable companies. 

Caesar is not Artificial-Intelligence, its not the president of the United States, its not the billionaire class, it’s not our digital technology, and its not even The Machine or The System. 

No— conceptually, Caesar is, whatever happens to be the most dominant system you find yourself conscripted into.
Which means, at the time of this writing, Caesar can only be the Stock Market.

It’s the one thing which all these “false Caesar’s” are subject to. Without appealing to The Stock Market Artificial Intelligence would run out of funding and would cease to exist. Without finding mass market appeal, digital technology would be a fringe, nerdish interest. Without inking deals with companies beholden to quarterly returns and positive balance statements The President of the United States and all of the other rank-and-file politicians might actually be usurped by people who give a damn. 

All these sub-Systems and “false Caesar’s” are compelled to do evil by The Stock Market, and everywhere you go The Stock Market is convincing you to join it. To pay it. To invest in it. To seek assistance navigating it. What’s more The Stock market is inescapable, no matter what you do, no matter where you go all money flows back into the Stock Market just as all roads lead to Rome. 

Luckily, just as there was no call placed upon on the ancient church to destroy Rome and assassinate Caesar, there is no impetus to destroy The Stock Market, and banish the billionaires. In fact, perhaps the most subversive thing we can do is pay The Stock Market what it’s owed and to get back to following the wisps and whims of the force which rules above and beyond our current Caesar.
