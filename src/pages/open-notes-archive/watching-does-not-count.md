Content styles like vlogs, reactions, and reviews are extremely popular and it seems like we’ve become satisfied with consuming, as opposed to experiencing both the novel and mundane. In practice, we are content with trading the ability to experience a new restaurant, purchase a good, or visit a landmark for the ability to watch someone else do it. We scratch the itch by peering into the lives of others and become satisfied or unexcited about what we had in mind.

To some extent watching reviews, or seeing the downsides of a potential plan can be helpful. Unfortunately, some of life itself is found in the process, in preparing, in experiencing an outcome that was a monumental disappointment.

What's more, our voyeuristic tendencies apply to more than what we purchase or where we choose to travel. It seems we are beginning to become convinced that watching is the same as doing. By and large we seem to think that clicking a like button, subscribing, or sharing a bit of content equates to a civic duty or is the same as toiling for things we believe in. Unfortunately, watching doesn’t count and viewing someone argue against a political regime or protest a certain policy is not the same as doing it yourself. Change doesn’t magically manifest as a result of people wishing it would or watching content from people who talk about how great it would be.

Unless we become motivated to participate in ways that go beyond the bezels of our screens it's unlikely we’ll actually see the things we vicariously cheer on made real. Media tycoons would have us believe that watching is the same as doing, but unfortunately (for them) we inhabit a physical world and tactile progress is still the prevalent purveyor of actually getting things done.
